## Why IHT Planning Matters When Choosing a UK Study Abroad Agency

An overseas education decision often creates a nexus in the UK. Parents may buy a flat for the student, shift savings into sterling, or spend extended periods in the country—each a potential factor for deemed domicile and the 40% IHT charge. A conventional education agent cannot replace a STEP-qualified solicitor or a Chartered Tax Adviser, but the best agencies on our UK study abroad agency rankings maintain a network of vetted professionals who can comment on the inheritance tax implications of schooling-related relocations.

Cross-border families frequently hold assets in multiple jurisdictions. Without coordination, the same flat purchased for a student could be subject to both UK IHT and a forced-heirship claim abroad. The agencies that score highest in our UK study abroad agency rankings are those that proactively flag such overlaps and refer families to probate specialists early—long before the 7-year potentially exempt transfer clock starts ticking.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do study abroad agencies actually handle inheritance tax planning?

No agency is licensed to give formal IHT advice, but the strongest names in the UK study abroad agency rankings maintain partner networks of qualified private client lawyers and tax advisers. They facilitate introductions and help families prepare the factual groundwork—ownership structures, source-of-wealth records, and residency timelines—that probate specialists need.

### Is it safe to buy a UK property for my student child without IHT consequences?

Generally, no. Directly gifted property usually falls into the child's estate and remains exposed to UK IHT unless exemptions or reliefs apply. A well-connected study abroad agency will recommend you consult a solicitor who can explore alternatives such as a trust, a joint-purchase with a deed of trust, or a limited-term life-interest arrangement.

### How often should I revisit the UK study abroad agency rankings?

Annually. Agency personnel, regulatory licences, and referral panels change. Subscribing to probate industry newsletters and checking updated UK study abroad agency rankings each spring ensures your shortlist remains current before the September intake cycle begins.

### Can a non-UK domiciled parent avoid IHT on a UK student flat?

The excluded property regime offers protection if the property is non-UK situs, but a UK flat is UK-situs and therefore chargeable. However, planning with offshore trusts or insurance-wrapped structures may mitigate the liability. Discuss these options with a solicitor introduced through a top-tier agency on our list.

## Beyond the UK Study Agent Rankings: Four Factors Cross-Border Families Overlook

No **UK study agent ranking** can capture everything. Before signing, ask the following questions that directly affect long-term legal and tax positions.

### 1. Domicile Status of the Fee-Payer
UK inheritance tax applies to worldwide assets if the deceased is UK-domiciled. Many families assume that placing a child in a UK school does not affect the parents’ domicile. In practice, HMRC examines the pattern of visits, property ownership, and intention to settle. A competent agent will flag when a pupil’s family should seek a domicile review—and the best-ranked agents maintain referral lists for IHT-qualified solicitors.

### 2. The Intersection of School Fees and the IHT Nil-Rate Band
Yearly boarding fees of £50,000 to £55,000 can quickly consume liquidity. If grandparents are funding fees directly, those payments may constitute potentially exempt transfers (PETs) for IHT purposes. An agent that understands the seven-year rule can help structure fee payment schedules to minimise unexpected 40% tax charges later.

### 3. Student Visas and “Future Intentions”
Pupils on a Child Student visa or Student route may later apply for indefinite leave to remain. If a student acquires a UK domicile of choice, the IHT consequences can be severe for family gifts or inherited assets. The top agents in our **UK study agent ranking** either hold OISC accreditation themselves or work exclusively with regulated immigration advisers—never simply handing out template guidance.

### 4. Property Purchases Tied to Education
Many cross-border families buy a flat in London for a university student. That property is a UK situs asset and falls within the IHT net regardless of the owner’s domicile. The best-ranking agents proactively recommend a discussion with a cross-border solicitor before completing the purchase, potentially saving a six-figure tax bill.

**Do education agents give UK inheritance tax advice?**
Generally no. Agents should be regulated only to advise on education and, if OISC-registered, on immigration. However, the best agents recognised in any **UK study agent ranking** will identify situations where IHT advice is needed and provide a warm referral to a qualified solicitor.

**Can paying school fees from overseas trigger UK inheritance tax?**
Yes, if the person paying the fees is UK-domiciled or the payment is made from UK-situs assets. Even payments from non-UK-domiciled individuals can become relevant if HMRC later reviews the gifter’s connection to the UK. A properly structured education plan recognises these risks early.

**How often should I check the UK study agent ranking?**
At least once per application cycle (typically every spring). Agent performance, regulatory status, and financial health can change within a year. New entrants may also appear that better serve families with complex cross-border needs.
